Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: July 11, 2026

BSD Custom Cabinetry is committed to making geezfully.com/bsd accessible to the widest possible audience, including people with disabilities, in line with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and applicable California accessibility law, including the Unruh Civil Rights Act.

1. Conformance Target

We are working toward conformance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA, the generally accepted standard for web accessibility. This is an ongoing effort, and this statement reflects our current status rather than a claim of full, certified conformance.

This statement describes our intent and ongoing effort. It is not a guarantee of full compliance. A full accessibility conformance audit should be performed by a qualified accessibility specialist before this claim is relied upon in a legal context.

2. Measures We Take

  • Semantic HTML structure with a logical heading hierarchy
  • Descriptive alt text for meaningful images
  • Visible focus states for keyboard navigation
  • Sufficient color contrast for body text against its background
  • Respect for the "prefers-reduced-motion" setting for visitors sensitive to animation
  • A responsive layout that works across screen sizes and zoom levels

3. Known Limitations

Despite our efforts, some parts of this Site may not yet be fully accessible. Known areas we are actively improving include full keyboard operability of the project gallery lightbox and ensuring all interactive elements meet minimum touch-target sizing on mobile. If you encounter a barrier not listed here, please let us know using the contact information below.

4. Feedback

If you experience any difficulty accessing content on this Site, please contact us. We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 5 business days and will work with you to provide the information or service you need through an alternative method if necessary.

5. Compatibility

This Site is designed to be compatible with recent versions of major browsers (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge) and commonly used assistive technologies, including screen readers such as VoiceOver and NVDA.
